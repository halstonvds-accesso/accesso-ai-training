
A comprehensive glossary of terms used across Accesso products including Passport, Siriusware, ShoWare, TE2, and Virtual Queuing.

---

## A–C

**Access Control**  
The function of specifying access rights to resources—in other words, who has access to what. Access control is commonly determined by ticket scanning and pass validation.

**ACE (Accesso Client eSupport)**  
An online portal that allows clients to send various requests to the Accesso Client Services team and track request progress. Clients use ACE for updates to stores or ticket products such as ticket price changes, new product setup, new user setup, and report requests.

**AP / A/P (Accounts Payable)**  
An accounting term reflecting money that the company owes to others. For example, a venue with a cancelled event would carry an A/P in the ledger until all customers were refunded.

**API (Application Program Interface)**  
A set of routines, protocols, and tools for building software applications. Accesso's open API allows applications to connect directly to any system with a supported API (integrated systems include Six Flags Ticketing System, aka SFTS).

**APP (Advanced Purchase Pricing)**  
Functionality where guests receive a discount based on how far in advance a ticket or tickets are purchased. Primarily used by Merlin Entertainments Group.

**App ID** _(TE2)_  
The string identifier for the client application build, consisting of the brand ID and app version number (e.g., "ST_0.0.1").

**App User ID** _(TE2)_  
The individual identifier for a specific app installation on a device used by the TE2 platform.

**AR / A/R (Accounts Receivable)**  
An accounting term reflecting money that others owe to the company. For example, a customer upgrading a $50 day pass to a $150 season pass would have an A/R of $100; a guest following a monthly payment plan for a $200 membership would reflect an updated, decreasing A/R each month until the balance was paid off.

**Authentication**  
The process of identity verification—proving a user is who they say they are.

**Authorization**  
The function of specifying access rights to resources (also known as access control). In other words, who has access to what.

**AWS (Amazon Web Services)**  
Cloud computing platform used by Accesso.

**Basic Retail** _(Siriusware)_  
Retail items that are always the same—for example, items that are not available in different sizes or colors.

**Beacon** _(TE2)_  
A low-powered Bluetooth transmitter that can be associated with a POI, represented by a UUID, major and minor IDs. Can represent a specific location and/or trigger an event upon discovery.

**BI Feed (Business Intelligence Feed)**  
The process of delivering business intelligence or information about business operations; external feed of data (typically runs through an FTP site).

**BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy)** _(Virtual Queuing)_  
Describes the Base Stations used for wearable firmware updates and can be deployed for wearable detection purposes.

**BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later)**  
A form of season pass payment plan in which a guest pays nothing at the time of purchase and agrees to pay at a set future date. The pass is not actually valid for admission until the final payment is completed.

**BOCA Printer**  
Brand of thermal ticket printer (based in Boca Raton, FL) used by several Accesso clients to produce tickets onsite.

**Calendar** _(TE2)_  
A Calendar manages schedules and acts as a system for organizing and grouping activities based on a timetable.

**Cart Abandonment**  
When a potential guest has added items to the eCommerce store Cart but then closed the store without completing the purchase. Passport offers a service that automatically sends emails to guests who abandoned items in the Cart, asking if they would like to return to complete the purchase.

**Charac (Characteristic)** _(Passport)_  
A name key-value pair tied to another concept in Accesso's online network system, used to trigger special functionality regarding that concept. Package characs allow you to add override information to a particular package. These are added via Passport Control and control various features in Shopland. Some are required for functionality, some are optional (e.g., store functionality like merchant, package).

**CipherLab Device**  
An Android device primarily used for scanning access codes. Devices are used across multiple products, including Passport iTracker, Siriusware Quick Apps, and ShoWare.

**Comp Ticket**  
A ticket that can be $0.00 or of monetary value. Can be attached to a full price offer (e.g., "Buy four admission tickets, get one free parking pass") or stand alone in the store, hidden under a promo code (e.g., "Enter promo code 'Comp' to receive one free ticket").

**Cross-Sell** _(Passport)_  
Suggesting other packages that would go well with the package currently being purchased.

**CSM (Client Success Manager)**  
Responsible for owning and developing the relationship with clients; serves as the primary contact on all aspects of Accesso eCommerce & POS account services.

**CSR (Client Service Representative)**  
An Accesso employee (LoQueue) assigned as a client contact for setting up packages and resolving issues that impede customer purchases either online or at the venue.

**CSS**

1. **Client Service Specialist** – An Accesso employee (Enterprise) assigned as a specific client contact for setting up packages and resolving issues that impede customer purchases either online or at the venue.
2. **Cascading Style Sheets** – A style sheet language used for describing the presentation of a document written in a markup language; a simple mechanism for adding style (e.g., fonts, colors, spacing) to web documents.

**CUP (China Union Pay)**  
Also known as Union Pay. A payment processor and the only domestic bank card organization in the People's Republic of China (PRC); founded in 2002.

**CyberSource**  
A third-party global payment processing service.

## D–H

**DB (Database)**  
A collection of information organized so that it can easily be accessed, managed, and updated. Databases can be classified according to types of content: bibliographic, full-text, numeric, and images.

**DCI (Departments, Categories, and Items)** _(Siriusware)_  
Items used throughout several applications within Siriusware Control. Items can be allocated into specific departments and categories for both eCommerce and retail use.

**Dev (Development Department)**  
Accesso's Development Department consisting of 4 teams:

- **Client User Interface (UI) Team (Front end)** – Constructs and maintains multiple Accesso applications, providing an intuitive interface for both clients and their customers to perform package purchases as well as manage accounts; includes Point of Sale (POS), mobile POS, Support Manager (SM), Payment Portal and Admin Console.
- **Consumer User Interface (UI) Team (Front end)** – Creates and maintains a seamless online, mobile and Point of Sale (POS) store interface for customers to quickly locate and purchase packages as well as manage membership accounts.
- **Core System Team (Backend)** – Develops and maintains database configurations and secure payment processor procedures.
- **Client Production Team (Backend)** – Supports and ensures network infrastructure stability; generates and maintains technical documentation for various Accesso groups.

**DFU (Device Firmware Update)** _(Virtual Queuing)_  
An operation to update the firmware on a device. Can be done wirelessly on certain devices.

**Docent** _(Siriusware)_  
Refers to a volunteer that performs duties during an event. In a museum setting, this can be a tour guide, educator, greeter, etc.

**DPR (Dynamic Pricing Rules)**  
Configurations that allow products to be sold at different price points on different dates or different times on the same date.

**EMV (Europay, MasterCard and Visa)**  
Refers to chip and PIN (Personal Identification Number) payment method.

**Entitlement**

- _(Siriusware, Passport)_ – The right or benefit assigned to a user, often in the form of a ticket for gaining access to events, shows, rides, attractions, and/or other limited availability products. An entitlement can transition through multiple statuses throughout its lifetime as it interfaces with other services to perform payment, assignment, and redemption flows.
- _(Virtual Queuing)_ – The entitlements feature is designed as a general-purpose framework for giving something to a Qsmart guest that they can then redeem at the moment of their choice (for example, a service level session and ride allowance).

**Event**

- _(Distribution)_ – A tour, attraction, or theater performance available for sale on third-party sites (such as Google, Groupon, Expedia, and more) through the Ingresso distribution system.
- _(Passport)_ – Used to determine anything that is date- or time-based with a package. Every package is tied to an event, even those that are not date- or time-based. For those that are, the dates and times are populated from a calendar.
- _(ShoWare)_ – A collection of one or more performances that exist under one umbrella. An event is the overarching "what", while a performance is the "when, where, and how much". For example, an Event can be a Film Festival with 10 different showings (1 event, 10 performances), a 3-week run of a Broadway title with 30 different showtimes (1 event, 30 performances), or a rugby playoff game (1 event, 1 performance).
- _(Siriusware)_ – An instance that appears in the calendar. Events are what are "booked" in the Resource Scheduling module. An event is a reservation of a resource at a specific location. An event is always of a certain event type.

**Event Types** _(Siriusware)_  
Also called calendar schedule groups. Event types are activities such as a tour or game. An event is a specific instance of an event type. Event types are not scheduled but events of a certain event type are. Each event type is associated with one or more resources.

**Extra Movie Control (XMC) / Extra Flow Control (XFC)** _(Passport)_  
Legacy terms from the era of Flash-based applications where components within an application needed separate files with a .swf extension, commonly referred to as "movies". Represents a set of configuration options for a given Extra Movie Control (XMC) or Extra Flow Control (XFC). Example: season_pass_select – the Season Pass module configured to collect passholder demographics.

**F&B (Food and Beverage)**  
Food and beverage operations.

**FDMS (First Data Merchant Services)**  
Global payment technology solutions company.

**FGL (Friendly Ghost Language)**  
Tags used for formatting; Boca's industry standard language that allows the user to print data, graphics, boxes, and bar codes anywhere on the ticket in virtually any orientation.

**GL Codes (General Ledger Codes)**  
A financial code used for accounting to sort different debit and credit entries to make reporting easier and more functional. Commonly used for recognizing revenue on a given ticket or experience. GL codes are included in BI feeds so venues can correctly allocate revenue and financial information.

**HTML (Hyper Text Markup Language)**  
The standard markup language used to create web pages. Along with CSS and JavaScript, HTML is a cornerstone technology used by most websites to create visually engaging webpages, user interfaces for web applications, and user interfaces for many mobile applications.

## I–N

**IAAPA (International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions)**  
Founded in 1918 for members to learn about new products, disseminate current industry news and increase operational profitability. Represents over 4,300 amusement industry members in over 97 countries. Accesso participates at each year's conference held in Orlando, FL with a demonstration booth.

**Identity** _(TE2)_  
An Identity represents a user account and profile allowing brands to connect with guests to better understand their needs and deliver seamless personalized experiences.

**Insufficient Balances**  
When a provided payment method does not have enough funding to complete the transaction.

**Jira**  
Project tracking software (created by Atlassian) that Accesso uses to track product development.

**Keyword** _(Passport)_  
A descriptor used to group packages together on a merchant.

**KIS (Kiosk Information Systems)**  
Hardware partner providing kiosks for several merchant locations with an intuitive user interface for streamlined and efficient guest experiences.

**Localization**  
Provides a repository for translations, letting users see strings in different languages, regions, and locales.

**Max4Sale** _(Siriusware)_  
A capability used to control item capacity, which allows sales configuration to include or exclude specific counts.

**Merchant** _(Passport)_  
A channel through which packages are sold. One venue can have multiple merchants, one for each sales channel. For example, "Accessoland" theme park could have Accessoland eCommerce, Accessoland POS, Accessoland mPOS, Accessoland Affiliates, and other merchants.

**MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)**  
Adds another layer of security when accessing systems by requiring users to provide an additional authentication factor such as a PIN (Personal Identification Number), password, or security token (i.e., a single-use PIN).

**Movie** _(Passport)_  
A legacy term from the era of Flash-based applications where components within an application needed separate files with a .swf extension, commonly referred to as "movies". Represents a set of configuration options for a given Extra Movie Control (XMC) or Extra Flow Control (XFC). Example: season_pass_select – the Season Pass module configured to collect passholder demographics.

**mPOS (Mobile Point of Sale)**  
Secure credit card processing, electronic signature capture, and on-demand ticket printing using Apple's iPad and iPhone. Devices include Linea, Honeywell (Shift4) and VeriFone US.

**Navigate to**  
Use the phrase "navigate to" instead of "go to" in Freedom documentation.

**NetOps (Network Operations)**  
An email alert method to notify a distribution list (also titled NetOps) when a backend application or process is experiencing an issue. The distribution list consists mostly of backend developers for monitoring on-call duties.

## O–Q

**OPS (Operations Team)**  
Provides 24-hour online, mobile and Point of Sale support for clients in terms of setting up packages and resolving issues that impede any outlet for customer purchases either online or at the client venue.

**Opt-in**  
A form of consent given by web users, commonly used to subscribe guests to email lists and promotions.

**Opt-out**  
Declining to participate and/or share contact information, usually related to venue email subscriptions and promotions.

**Package**

- _(Passport)_ – A product sold by a merchant. Each package in an order is considered a ticket.
- _(ShoWare)_ – A fixed collection of two or more performances, which share the same seat map, grouped into a single product for ticket sales. Customers receive the same seat to each performance in the package and pay a set price based on seating location. Package aliases often include "season subscription" or "season tickets."

**Passport Support** _(Passport)_  
A web-based client application that offers a reliable and responsive way to review and modify orders through a secure web-based system. Features include:

- Search for orders multiple ways including order number, name, phone number, last four digits of a credit card, and more
- Review and modify specific order details and elements
- Process refunds
- Reprint tickets
- Track guest inquiry history

**PC (Passport Control)**  
Admin tool that allows users to create and edit packages (tickets), merchants, users, affiliate stores, event calendars, and more for Passport clients.

**PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)**  
A proprietary information security standard for organizations that handle branded credit cards from the major card schemes including Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover, and JCB (Japan Credit Bureau). A set of requirements designed to ensure that all companies that process, store or transmit credit card information maintain a secure environment.

**PCL (Printer Command Language)**  
A page description language developed by Hewlett-Packard as a printer protocol that has become an industry standard.

**Performance** _(ShoWare)_  
A system entry defining "when, where, and how much", categorized under an Event. A Performance requires a unique, user-defined Performance Code for identification, and requires selection of an existing Box Office, Promoter, Venue, Seat Map, Event, and Shipping Method during setup.

**PHD (Production Help Desk)**  
Accesso's system for strategically escalating issues pertaining to the functionality of applications and services via digital tickets (see SLA for PHD ticket priority ratings).

**PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**  
Any data that could potentially identify a specific individual. Examples include name, address, email address, telephone number, birthdate, and more.

**PM (Project Manager)**  
A member of Accesso's Project Management team that provides an enterprise-side approach to identify, prioritize, and successfully execute initiatives and projects aligned with Accesso's strategic goals and vision.

**POI (Point of Interest)**  
A specific point location that someone may find useful or interesting.

- _(TE2)_ – A specific entity or setting within a venue with a physical location; may be associated to one or more beacons.

**POS (Point of Sale)**  
Offers streamlined front gate sales designed to minimize training and increase speed of transactions at Accesso's client locations. Supports Group Sales, Season Pass Processing, Tracker and Reservations.

**Points4Sale** _(Siriusware)_  
A capability used to control item capacity, which can draw inventory from anywhere and does not tie to item quantity.

**PSE (Production Support Engineer)**  
A position on Accesso's Client Production Team that manages Production Help Desk tickets.

**QLM (Queue Length Measurement)** _(Virtual Queuing)_  
Refers to an Accesso-developed system for automatically measuring the length of a queue for greater accuracy in gauging ride throughput.

**QuickSell**  
A suggestion of an addition to a customer's cart on the same screen as the normal package.

## R–Z

**RDL (Report Definition Language)**  
An XML-based schema developed by Microsoft for defining reports. In addition to report layouts, Accesso Siriusware uses RDL for ticket layouts.

**Reservation** _(Siriusware)_  
A reservation associates a guest with a resource.

**Resource** _(Siriusware)_  
Also known as calendar schedule group items. These are traditional Salesware items, configured in the DCI section of Siriusware Control. Resources are what are sold in Resource Scheduling module. Resources are reserved for events and these events are then scheduled. One or more resources are associated with each event type. An event, then, is a sale of an instance of an event type and its associated resources.

**RNR (Reserve 'N Ride)**  
Merlin functionality that allows guests to reserve a spot to ride in-demand rides.

**ROI (Rate of Interest / Return on Investment)**

1. The interest rate applied to various forms of credit, also referred to as loanable funds.
2. Return on Investment.

**Sandbox**  
Test or staging environment for an application.

**SDET (Software Developer in Test)**  
A developer who, instead of working in the product development team, works as part of the test/QA team.

**SLA (Service Level Agreement)**  
The priority rating system that designates the urgency and expected resolution time of each ACE, PHD, or other support ticket or request. SLA timetables can vary between product lines and ticket types.

**SM (Support Manager)** _(Passport)_  
Passport's retired client application that offered a reliable and responsive way to review and modify orders through a secure web-based system. Replaced by Passport Support.

**SOR (System of Record)**  
A system which is the undisputed authority with regard to some set of information.

**SPROC (Stored Procedure)**  
Lines of SQL code that can be saved and reused.

**SQL (Structured Query Language)**  
A set of syntax and commands used to communicate with relational databases in order to review, update, add, or delete information stored in the database.

**SS** _(Siriusware)_  
Siriusware Support.

**Stream Processor** _(TE2)_  
Provides administrative functions for managing event streams into the platform including rule definitions and processing windows.

**SVC (Stored Value Card)**  
Cards sold by a merchant that can be used as a form of payment at the merchant's locations. Can either be used until the original value has been exhausted or more funds can be loaded onto the card by the consumer.

**SW (Accesso Siriusware)**  
An Accesso product line offering customizable point-of-sale systems and much more.

**SWAC (ShoWare Access Control)** _(ShoWare)_  
One of two scanning platforms used to validate ticket barcodes. See also Web Scanning.

**T&C (Terms & Conditions)**  
Terms for certain product payment plans that don't fall under the requirements for the Truth In Lending Act.

**TILA (Truth In Lending Act)**  
A 1968 U.S. federal consumer credit reform. A legal agreement that guests must sign when purchasing a deferred pass from a client, aka the "fine print."

**TMA (Team Member App)** _(Virtual Queuing)_  
A smartphone application used by Virtual Queuing that park operations staff use for guest recovery services as well as core operational functionality. Staff members can use it to make reservations on behalf of guests and validate guest entry to attractions. Ride attendants can also use the TMA to make fine adjustments to the buffer queue length.

**Upsell** _(Passport)_  
A suggestion of how a customer could upgrade the package they are currently purchasing in the eCommerce store. For example, a guest selecting a Daily Ticket could see an offer to Upsell to an Annual Pass.

**User Message** _(TE2)_  
The guest-facing version of a Message providing useful information while in and outside of the venue, such as general information, updates, and alerts, as well as greetings and farewells.

**User Tag** _(TE2)_  
Represents short or long-term contextual information, classifications, or history about a guest's activity. This dynamic tagging leverages data attributes and functions around existing account, profile, location, visit, and telemetry data, allowing you to efficiently determine relevant groups as target audiences.

**Vendor** _(Passport)_  
An organization to which a group of merchants belong.

**Venue**

- _(TE2)_ – Representation of large-scale public or private setting; a set of POIs.
- _(ShoWare)_ – Location of an event; the performance space. Multiple venues can exist at the same physical address (e.g., theatre 1, theatre 2, arena, outdoor terrace, ballroom 1).

**V4/V5** _(Passport)_  
Versions of Accesso's Shopland online store application. V5 was introduced in 2013.

**WTP (Wireless Transaction Protocol)**  
Used with RFID cards at ski resorts. The microchip in an RFID card contains a unique serial number called a WTP number.

**XFC (Extra Flow Control)** _(Passport)_  
The modern definition for modules within Accesso's Shopland V5, such as Season Pass, Payment Plan, and Zoo Memberships.

**XMC (Extra Movie Control)** _(Passport)_  
A legacy definition for modules within Accesso's Shopland V4 online store platform.
